# AAPL — Apple Inc.

**Sector:** Consumer electronics & services (hardware + software ecosystem)
**Market cap:** ~$3.0 trillion (June 2026, approximate)
**Added:** 2026-06-14
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-14
**Score:** 6/10 — exceptional business, but priced like everyone already knows it; the setup is fine, not a bargain

## What they do
Apple sells premium consumer hardware — iPhone above all, plus Mac, iPad, Watch, and AirPods — and the iPhone is still the engine, generating roughly half of revenue. The trick that makes Apple Apple isn't any single gadget, it's that once you own one, the others get more convenient and switching away gets more annoying. That's the moat: not a patent, but the friction of leaving (your photos, your messages, your apps, your watch that only pairs with your phone).

The more interesting half of the story is Services — the App Store cut, iCloud storage, Apple Pay, advertising, and a fat recurring payment from Google to stay the default search engine. Services is now a very large, high-margin, recurring chunk of profit that grows faster than hardware and smooths out the lumpy phone-upgrade cycle. So think of Apple as a hardware company that has quietly bolted on a software-toll business riding on top of ~2 billion active devices.

## Why interesting
It's one of the best businesses on earth: enormous free cash flow, pricing power, a loyal installed base, and a management team that returns oceans of cash via buybacks (steadily shrinking the share count, which juices per-share earnings even when the company barely grows). If you want a fortress that compounds slowly and rarely blows up, this is close to the platonic ideal.

## Risks
The flip side of "everyone knows it's great" is that the stock is priced for greatness — you're paying a premium multiple for fairly modest unit growth, so a lot of good news is already in the price. Real risks: iPhone growth has basically plateaued in mature markets, China is both a big revenue source and a big geopolitical/competitive wildcard, and the lucrative Google search payment is squarely in regulators' crosshairs — losing or shrinking it would dent the Services story. Apple has also looked like a follower, not a leader, in the AI wave, which matters if the next computing platform reshuffles the deck. None of these is fatal; together they cap the upside.

## History

### 2026-06-14 — first look
What stands out is the quality-vs-price tension: this is arguably the highest-quality megacap there is, but at ~$3T and a rich multiple you're not being handed a margin of safety. The Services flywheel and relentless buybacks are the real reasons to own it; the flat iPhone unit growth, China exposure, the at-risk Google payment, and a lagging AI narrative are the reasons not to overpay. A 6 reflects "wonderful business, merely okay setup" — I'd want either a cheaper entry or clearer evidence Apple wins the next platform before scoring it higher.
